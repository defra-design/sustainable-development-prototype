Apply for a licence to affect badger setts

You can only apply if:

  • you have the landowner's permission
  • the project has got all permissions for work to start (for example, planning permission or listed building consent)

To be granted a licence to carry out the mitigation work, you must:

  • explain how the proposed development work will interfere with the badger setts
  • explain how youâ€™ll carry out any mitigation work
  • include copies of documents that demonstrate the work

The decision on whether to issue a licence should be made within 30 working days of receiving your application and supporting documents.

You will need to report the actions to show your compliance with the licence after you have carried out the work. Find out about reporting your compliance with a wildlife licence.

You may also need to pay for this licence. Find out about paying for a wildlife licence.

Before you start

You will need:

  • details of who will be responsible for the development work and will hold the licence
  • to be authorised to apply for the person who will hold the licence, if you are applying on their behalf
  • details of an ecologist experienced in carrying out mitigation work to minimise the effects on wildlife
  • a survey carried out by an ecologist, including precise mapping of the affected badger setts
  • a statement setting out the methods that will be used to minimise harm to or disturbance of the badgers
