Monitoring

The minimum expectations for your post-development monitoring depend on the species of bats present at the site and the types of roost.

About the minimum monitoring expectations

Day, night, feeding and transitional roosts

Check that the monitoring and management remain before submitting a report of action taken under the licence.

If there are horseshoe, Bechstein's, Barbastelle Grey long-eared, Leisler's, Alcathoe, Serotine, or Nathusius' pipistrelle bats, check that any retained features or compensation roosts remain in place at the end of the licence period and are suitable for the intended purpose. This check can be combined with the post-development monitoring inspection.

If monitoring, management or maintenance identify any issues, these should be fixed.

Satellite roosts

Check that any retained features or compensation roosts remain in place at the end of the licence period and are suitable for the intended purpose. This check can be combined with the post-development monitoring inspection.

If monitoring, management or maintenance identify any issues, these should be fixed.

Minor hibernation roosts

Immediately after development, you must make at least one inspection. This inspection must ensure the compensation is done in line with the site registration.

You must carry out at least 2 years of post-development monitoring. This should be done in the first hibernation season post-development, and then in year 3.

You should get evidence that the retained roost or compensation measures are suitable for, and are used for, hibernating.

If there are horseshoe, Bechstein's or more than 2 Barbastelle bats, your maintenance and monitoring must be done for 10 years.

Major hibernation roosts

Management and maintenance must been done for at least 10 years, with the last check made in the season that the licence expires.

Ensure the retained roost or compensation provided is fit for purpose, including airflow, temperature, light, humidity.

Fix any issues that affect the roost's use for hibernating that are found during monitoring, management or maintenance.

Small or medium maternity roosts

Management and maintenance must been done for 2 years, with the last check made in the season that the licence expires.

Ensure the retained roost or compensation provided is fit for purpose, including airflow, temperature, light, humidity.

Fix any issues that affect the roost's use for breeding that are found during monitoring, management or maintenance.

If there are horseshoe, Bechstein's or more than 2 Barbastelle bats, you maintenance and monitoring must be done for 10 years.

Large or very large maternity roosts

Management and maintenance must been done for at least 10 years, with the last check made in the season that the licence expires.

Ensure the retained roost or compensation provided is fit for purpose, including airflow, temperature, light, humidity.

Fix any issues that affect the roost's use for breeding that are found during monitoring, management or maintenance.
