Apply for and manage licences to affect badger setts as part of development

Use this service to apply for a licence to interfere with badger setts as part of development work. You can also use the service to manage these licences and report action you have taken under the licence.

Development work covered by this licence includes:

  • housing developments
  • laying pipelines
  • building new fences

You can only apply if:

  • you have the landowner's permission for the development work
  • the project has got all permissions for work to start (for example, planning permission or listed building consent)

To be granted a licence to carry out work that affects badger setts, you must:

  • explain how the proposed development work will interfere with the badger setts
  • explain how you’ll carry out any work to mitigate the impact of the development on badgers
  • include copies of documents that demonstrate the work you intend to do

The decision on whether to grant a licence should be made within 30 working days of Natural England receiving your application.

Paying for a licence

You will need to pay for this licence, unless your project is exempt or a charge does not apply to your activity. The information you give when using the service will allow Natural England to decide:

  • whether you need to pay for the licence
  • how much you’ll pay for the licence

You can enter a purchase order number in the service.

If Natural England decides that you need to pay for a licence, it will send you an invoice and details of how to pay.

Read the terms and conditions for paying for a wildlife licence.

Before you start

You will need:

  • a Government Gateway account
  • a Defra account
  • details of who will be responsible for the development work and will hold the licence
  • to be authorised to apply for the person who will hold the licence, if you are applying on their behalf
  • details of an ecologist experienced in carrying out mitigation work to minimise the effects on wildlife
  • a survey carried out by an ecologist, including precise mapping of the affected badger setts before, during and after development work
  • a statement setting out the methods that will be used to minimise harm to or disturbance of the badgers

If you need to apply for a licence but are unable to use digital services, email xxxxx@assisted_digital_email.

Existing applications and licences

You can sign in to your Defra account view your granted licences or continue an existing licence application.

Reporting action taken under the licence

You can use this service to send a report of action taken under a licence.

You must submit a report of action taken under the licence within 14 working days of a licence expiring. You must submit a report of action even if you did not do any of the activities you were licensed to carry out.
