Exemptions from paying for a wildlife licence

You may not need to pay for a wildlife licence if the purpose of your licence is to:

  • preserve public health and safety
  • prevent damage to livestock, crops, timber or property
  • prevent disease spreading
  • be home improvement through householder planning permission or permitted development
  • be for scientific, research or educational purposes
  • be for conservation of protected species, including protection of bat roosts
  • be to conserve scheduled monuments, listed buildings, places of worship, or traditional farm buildings
