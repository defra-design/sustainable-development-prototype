Exemptions from paying for a wildlife licence

You may not need to pay for a wildlife licence if:

  • the purpose of your licence is to preserve public health and safety
  • the purpose of your licence is to prevent damage to livestock, crops, timber or property
  • your project is to improve your home through householder planning permission or permitted development
  • the main aim of the project is science, research or education related to the protected species or protected site
  • the main aim of the project is conservation of the protected species
  • the main aim of the project is to conserve a scheduled monument, listed building, place of worship or traditional farm building in a stewardship agreement
